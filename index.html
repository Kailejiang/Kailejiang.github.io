Biography

I am a master's student specializing in the intersection of machine learning and physical chemistry. My research primarily focuses on developing novel machine learning models that go beyond traditional approaches to bridge the gap between machine learning model and physical chemistry principles.Using interdisciplinary methods to enhance the interpretability of machine learning models is also one of the key focuses of my present and future research.

The research results include CLRNet, a machine learning potential energy surface (PES) fitting model that can effectively accommodate high-energy structures. In addition, I also explored a method combining transfer learning with causal analysis to conduct interpretability analysis on machine learning models.

My work has been published in renowned journals such as J. Chem. Inf. Model. and J. Chem. Phys. I have also received several achievements, including a national scholarship and a national invention patent.

Before 2023, my study focus was on deep learning and computer vision and their applications in medical devices. I obtained my bachelor's degree in Optical and Electronic Information Engineering in 2023, with a thesis entitled "Surgical Instrument Recognition Based on Improved YOLO."

June 15, 2025 Machine Learning

Recent Advances in Transfer Learning for Molecular Properties Prediction

In recent months, I've been exploring how transfer learning techniques can be applied to molecular properties prediction. Traditional machine learning models often struggle with limited data in specific chemical domains, but by leveraging pre-trained models on large datasets, we can significantly improve performance on smaller, more specialized datasets.

My latest work demonstrates that a model pre-trained on quantum mechanical data can be fine-tuned to predict reaction barriers with much higher accuracy than models trained from scratch. This approach not only saves computational resources but also provides better generalization across chemical spaces.

May 22, 2025 Research Methods

Designing Interpretable ML Models for Chemical Insights

One of the biggest challenges in applying machine learning to chemistry is the lack of interpretability. Black-box models might achieve high accuracy, but they fail to provide insights that can advance chemical understanding.

In my recent work, I've been developing hybrid models that combine physical priors with neural networks. These models not only maintain high predictive performance but also allow us to extract meaningful chemical insights. For example, by analyzing attention weights in our model, we can identify which atomic interactions are most critical for a particular reaction property.

April 8, 2025 Conference Report

Highlights from the 2025 ML4Chemistry Conference

Last month, I attended the ML4Chemistry conference in Berlin, which brought together researchers from around the world working at the intersection of machine learning and chemistry. The conference featured exciting developments in several areas, including active learning for autonomous experimentation and graph neural networks for molecular design.

I presented our work on "Transfer Learning with Physical Constraints for Reaction Prediction," which generated interesting discussions about balancing physical interpretability with predictive performance. It was particularly encouraging to see increasing interest in methods that go beyond pure prediction to provide chemical insights.
